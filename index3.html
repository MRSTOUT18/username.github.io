INTRODUCTION TO THE INTERNET

The Internet consists of a vast array of interconnected computer networks. Each network can connect dozens, hundreds, or even thousands of computers, allowing them to share data and computational resources. The Internet enables people worldwide to communicate effectively and affordably. Unlike conventional broadcast media like radio and television, the Internet lacks a centralized distribution system. Instead, anyone with Internet access can engage directly with others online, disseminate information publicly, access information, utilize remote applications and services, or conduct commerce.
